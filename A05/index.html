Exploring Jeonju: A Photo Journey

Jeonju is a historic and culturally rich city located in North Jeolla Province, Jeonbuk, southwestern South Korea. As of 2025, its metropolitan area has an estimated population of 668,000.

Historical Significance

Jeonju’s origins trace back to the Three Kingdoms period (c. 57 BCE–668 CE), serving as a strategic center for civil and military administration. During the Unified Silla period, it was known as Wansanju. In the Later Three Kingdoms era, it became the capital of the short-lived Later Baekje kingdom (892–936 CE).

Cultural Highlights

The city is renowned for the Jeonju Hanok Village, which features over 800 traditional Korean hanok houses, offering a glimpse into Korea’s architectural heritage. Jeonju is also the birthplace of bibimbap and has been designated a UNESCO City of Gastronomy.

Geography and Climate

Situated approximately 34 km east of the Yellow Sea, Jeonju is surrounded by hills and mountains, providing a scenic backdrop. The city experiences a temperate climate with mild springs and autumns, making it an ideal destination year-round.

Education and Transportation

Jeonju hosts several higher education institutions, including Chonbuk National University and Jeonju University. The city is well–connected via the Honam Expressway and is accessible from Gunsan Airport, located about an hour away by shuttle.
